The wedge isn't DORA metrics.
It's AI governance.

Every VP Eng already has a dashboard. What they don't have is an answer to two simultaneous board-level questions.

Engineering leaders at mid-market and enterprise software companies are under pressure from two directions at once. From the board: "What ROI are we getting from GitHub Copilot, Cursor, Codeium?" From security and compliance: "Are we introducing AI-generated vulnerabilities into production?"

Most existing platforms (LinearB, Jellyfish, DX) answer DORA metrics but have no AI governance layer. That gap is Truxt's clearest wedge. The outbound motion I'd build leads with AI governance and ROI, not deployment frequency, because governance creates urgency whereas another dashboard does not.

02. ICP

Three segments.
Two to close in 60 days.

Prioritized by likelihood to close and quality of product fit, not just size.

● Priority 1
Mid-market SaaS
100–800 employees, US-based
  • Already paying for Copilot or Cursor enterprise-wide, under pressure to justify the spend
  • VP Eng or Eng Director owns the tooling P&L
  • GitHub + Jira + CI/CD stack. Truxt integrates cleanly.
  • Decision cycle: 4–8 weeks, single buyer or small committee
● Priority 1
AI-first engineering orgs
High AI code output by design
  • AI-assisted development is core to their product velocity
  • Board is asking about AI ROI. CTO is the economic buyer.
  • Often lack any structured governance, which is the primary value prop
  • Strong urgency, fast decision timeline
◎ Priority 2
Enterprise DevOps teams
500+ engineers, longer cycle
  • Rolling out AI coding tools at scale across large eng orgs
  • Compliance, security, audit requirements amplify governance urgency
  • Longer cycle (3–6 months) but higher ACV
  • Start conversations now, close in Q3–Q4

04. Most creative campaign

Give something useful
before you ask for anything.

This is the P.S. your JD asked for, and the philosophy behind why this page exists instead of a cover letter.

↑ Personalisation at scale → higher response rate

At Nomad eSIM, I was breaking into fintech BD contacts across Southeast Asia without a recognizable brand name behind me. Instead of leading with a product pitch, I created a personalized landing page for each prospective account, highlighting the specific benefits of an eSIM partnership tailored to their user base, geography, and product context.

The approach drew on market data showing how comparable fintechs in their region were already monetizing travel connectivity for their users, something prospects couldn't easily find on their own. No generic deck. No Nomad branding upfront. Just intelligence that was directly relevant to them.

The result: meaningfully higher response rates and a level of personalization that made prospects feel understood before a single call happened.

The lesson I carried forward: the most effective first move in B2B outbound is often to give something genuinely useful before you ask for anything. It repositions you from vendor to peer. The same logic applies here. This page is that move.

05. First 30 days

What I'd actually do,
week by week.

Not a generic onboarding checklist. Specific actions tied to the actual product and ICP.

Week 1–2
Learn & calibrate
Shadow Naveen on sales calls to understand how the product is currently positioned and where buyers push back
Audit existing outreach and pipeline (if any). The goal is to identify patterns in who responded, who converted, who ghosted
Map the 3 sharpest objections and draft responses: "We have LinearB," "GitHub already shows us this," "My devs won't adopt another tool"
Build Apollo + Sales Nav account list: 200 qualified accounts. Filter for companies with active Copilot or Cursor job postings as a proxy for AI coding adoption.
Week 3–4
Launch & iterate
Launch sequence to first 60 accounts and track open rates, reply rates, positive response rate daily in CRM
Run 3–5 discovery calls. Note every objection, question, and moment of genuine interest. Feed back to Naveen as positioning intelligence.
A/B test subject lines: "AI-generated code governance at [Company]" vs "What ROI are you seeing from Copilot?" and kill the underperformer after 40 sends
Propose one content piece based on a real buyer insight, specifically a LinkedIn post or short benchmark Truxt can own
Month 2
Scale what works
Expand to 100+ new accounts per week using the validated sequence
Start working Segment C (enterprise) in parallel with longer timelines in mind
Build a referral ask into the discovery call flow for warm intros to peers
Weekly pipeline review: accounts touched, replies, demos booked, pipeline value, conversion rate per segment
